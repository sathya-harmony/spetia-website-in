## 0. Read this section first. The single most important rule.

**Default to subtraction.**

If a section, sentence, image, animation, color, divider, icon, or button could be removed without breaking the user's path from arrival to "MESSAGE STUDIO SPETIA," remove it. The website you are building is for a Bangalore plot owner, age 40-55, who is reading this on his phone between meetings. He wants to know who Studio Spetia is, what they build, where they have built it, and how to reach them. Anything else is decoration. Decoration reads as insecurity. Whitespace is the design.

This is the first rule because it is the rule most violated by AI builders. Read it twice.

## 1. The story we are telling (the spine of the website)

Every page on this website is in service of one story. Read this story carefully. It is the spine. Every word of copy, every image choice, every layout decision must feed this story or it does not belong on the site.

**The story:**

> Sangeetha is a Bangalore architect with twenty years of practice and eighty homes built across the city. Her son Sathya, an engineer trained at NIT Karnataka, came back to the city to run the studio with her. Together, the architect and the engineer build homes in Bangalore — design and construction held by the same studio, the same hands, the same family.
>
> The work is done in the master builder tradition. The architect draws and the engineer builds, but neither hands the work to a stranger in the middle. Sangeetha walks every site. Sathya runs the operations. They take six commissions a year. The pricing is shared in conversation, not on a website.

This is the story. The website is a quiet way of telling it. It is not loud. It is not a sales pitch. It is the studio's living room, opened to the visitor for four minutes.

**The mother-son framing is the heart of the brand.** It must come through in the photography (the two of them together), the voice (an architect-engineer practice spoken about in dignified third person), the work (eighty homes carries Sangeetha; the studio carries them both), and the contact moment (a WhatsApp message goes to a real mother and a real son, not a sales team).

## 2. Tone and voice — read this before writing a single word

Studio Spetia copy must be **clear English that an Indian reader understands at first pass, written with restraint that signals tier without using tier-words.** This is the single hardest part of the build. Most AI-generated copy fails here. Most architect websites fail here. Read this section before writing any line that appears on the site.

### 2.1 The four rules of the voice

1. **Specific over abstract.** Say "kota stone floor in the living room" not "premium materials." Say "eleven months from agreement to keys" not "an efficient timeline." Say "Indiranagar, Sarjapura, and Whitefield" not "leading neighborhoods." Specificity is the trust signal. Abstraction is the AI tell.

2. **Short over long.** Average sentence: under 18 words. Average paragraph: under 50 words. If a sentence runs more than 24 words, break it. Indian readers across English fluency levels read short sentences faster and trust them more.

3. **Plain over clever.** A 45-year-old plot owner in HSR Layout whose English is functional but not native-fluent must understand every line on first pass. No idioms that don't translate. No English-major vocabulary. No metaphors that need unpacking. "We design and build homes in Bangalore" is the register. "Where craftsmanship meets soul" is not.

4. **Dignified third person for the founders.** Sangeetha and Sathya are spoken about with the dignity that twenty years of practice and a degree from NIT Karnataka deserve. They are not "studying," "passionate about," "young and dynamic," or "bringing fresh perspective." They are the principal architect of Studio Spetia and the engineer who runs it.

### 2.2 How to write Sangeetha (the architect)

She is the principal. She has twenty years of practice, eighty homes designed across Bangalore, and she walks every site. The copy treats her with the gravity that record carries.

**Correct:**

> Sangeetha is the principal architect of Studio Spetia. Twenty years of practice across Bangalore. Eighty homes. She walks every site.

> Sangeetha leads the design of every Studio Spetia home. She drew her first Bangalore home in Jayanagar twenty years ago and has since designed eighty more across the city.

> The architect on every Studio Spetia project is Sangeetha. She does not delegate the design.

**Incorrect (do not write any of this):**

> ~~Sangeetha is a passionate architect who loves crafting bespoke homes...~~

> ~~With over two decades of experience, Sangeetha brings a unique perspective...~~

> ~~Sangeetha believes that every home tells a story...~~

> ~~Sangeetha is a renowned architect known for her dedication to excellence...~~

### 2.3 How to write Sathya (the engineer)

He is the engineer who runs the studio. He trained at NIT Karnataka — one of India's most respected engineering institutes — and he runs operations, the build process, and the digital systems. He is **not** "studying" engineering. He is **not** "young and ambitious." He is the engineer of a working architecture practice, and the copy treats him as such.

**Correct:**

> Sathya is the engineer of Studio Spetia. NIT Karnataka, mechanical engineering. He runs the build.

> The construction side of every project is led by Sathya, an engineer trained at NIT Karnataka. He is on site every working day.

> Sathya runs the studio's operations, the build, and the systems that hold the work together. He is the engineer on every project.

**Incorrect:**

> ~~Sathya is currently studying mechanical engineering at NIT Karnataka...~~ (apologetic)

> ~~Sathya is a young engineer with a passion for construction...~~

> ~~Sathya brings fresh perspective and youthful energy to the practice...~~

**The framing rule:** if Sathya is graduating in 2027, that's an internal fact. The website does not need to say "studying." It says "engineer trained at NIT Karnataka." Both are true. The second is dignified.

### 2.4 The mother-son story (use sparingly, treat with dignity)

Two patterns work; everything else fails.

**Pattern A — As a fact, stated once, plainly:**

> Studio Spetia is run by a mother and a son. Sangeetha is the architect. Sathya is the engineer. Together they design and build homes in Bangalore.

**Pattern B — As a continuity, embedded in the work:**

> The studio is two practices held together. The architect's twenty years of Bangalore work, and the engineer's eye for what a building does after it is drawn. Mother and son. One studio.

**Incorrect:**

> ~~The dynamic mother-son duo is revolutionizing Bangalore architecture...~~
> ~~Combining decades of experience with youthful innovation...~~
> ~~A heartwarming story of family, tradition, and modern design...~~

The mother-son framing should appear three to four times across the site, in different contexts, never as a marketing hook.

### 2.5 The Indian-English calibration

The site is read by Bangaloreans whose English ranges from native-fluent to functional. The copy must work for both:

- **Simple, concrete words.** "Build" not "construct." "Plan" not "blueprint." "Drawing" not "schematic." "Site" not "location." "Home" not "residence."
- **Active voice.** "We walk the site" not "the site is walked by us."
- **Anglo-Saxon over Latinate.** "Start" not "commence." "End" not "conclude." "Help" not "assist."
- **No English-only idioms.** Do not use "the bigger picture," "the long haul," "from the ground up." These read as effortful in Indian English.
- **Specific Indian context is good.** "Soil bearing capacity," "BBMP approval," "Khata classification," "Sarjapura Road," "1500 square feet plot."
- **Numbers in words for small numbers.** "Six commissions a year" not "6 commissions a year." "Eighty homes" not "80 homes."

### 2.6 Banned words and phrases (final, exhaustive list)

The following appear nowhere on the site. Not in headlines, not in body, not in alt text, not in metadata, not in form labels, not in error messages.

**Marketing adjectives (banned):** premium, luxury, world-class, elite, exclusive, uncompromising, cutting-edge, innovative, seamless, holistic, best-in-class, leading, premier, renowned, top, finest, outstanding.

**Indian construction clichés (banned):** dream home, your dream, dream space, vision into reality, turning dreams into reality, world-class quality, international standards, state-of-the-art.

**Marketing verbs (banned):** transform, elevate, unleash, unlock, empower, redefine, revolutionize, reimagine, pioneer, craft (as verb), curate, deliver (as verb of value), realize (as in "realize your vision").

**AI-tell phrases (banned):**
- "It's not just X. It's Y."
- "Where X meets Y."
- "Whether you're X or Y, we..."
- "Crafting bespoke experiences"
- "Where vision meets reality"
- "Bringing your dreams to life"
- "Embark on a journey"
- "Reimagine your home"
- "Tailored to perfection"
- "Curated for the discerning"
- "From X to Y, we..."
- "In a world where..."
- "Imagine a home that..."
- "We don't just X — we Y"
- "Where every detail tells a story"
- "Designed with you in mind"
- "Your vision, our expertise"

**"Bespoke" as adjective (banned).** "Bespoke" is one of our three tier names (a proper noun). It never appears as a marketing adjective.

**Punctuation (banned):**
- Exclamation marks. Anywhere. Zero.
- Emoji. Anywhere. Zero.
- Em-dashes used as decorative pause more than once per 200 words.
- Triple ellipses for drama.

**Structural patterns (banned):**
- Rule of three: "We design. We build. We deliver." Banned.
- Action-verb headlines: "Build your dream." Banned.
- Question headlines: "Looking for an architect?" Banned.

### 2.7 Voice examples — full sentences and paragraphs

**Correct (use this register):**

> We design and build homes in Bangalore. The architect on every project is Sangeetha. The engineer is Sathya. The studio takes six commissions a year.

> Twenty years ago Sangeetha drew her first Bangalore home, in Jayanagar, on a drafting board with a 2H pencil. Eighty homes later, the work has not changed in the way that matters. The architect still walks the site. Sangeetha still picks the stone. She still signs off the column-marking before the slab is poured.

> The studio is small on purpose. Six homes a year is the number Sangeetha and Sathya can hold without losing the quality of the work. If you write to us in May for a project we cannot start until October, we will tell you in May.

> The wall on the south elevation went up twice. Once for the original structure, and once after the structural engineer's review. The teak in the front door came from a house being demolished in Malleshwaram.

**Incorrect (rewrite if you find anything like this):**

> ~~Welcome to Studio Spetia, where dreams are crafted into stunning architectural masterpieces by our passionate mother-son duo.~~

> ~~With over two decades of experience and an unwavering commitment to excellence, Sangeetha brings unparalleled expertise...~~

> ~~At Studio Spetia, we believe that every home tells a unique story.~~

> ~~Embark on a transformative journey with our bespoke design services tailored to your discerning lifestyle.~~

## 3. Strategic frame: hybrid Hormozi-editorial execution

Studio Spetia operates in the gap between Brick & Bolt's volume engine and Khosla Associates' silent-PR studio. The website resolves this by combining **Alex Hormozi's funnel mechanics** (single primary CTA on every page, qualifying funnel, friction reduction, real scarcity, video proof for trust-heavy moments) with **editorial design vocabulary** from Omai Villas (top-centered wordmark, eyebrow + lower-left serif headline, full-bleed cinematic photography, soft warm neutrals, simple line-input forms).

### 3.1 The Hormozi mechanics this site keeps

| Hormozi principle | Studio Spetia execution |
|---|---|
| Grand Slam Offer | "Architecture and construction under one studio. Six homes a year. The architect walks every site." Stated as fact, not as marketing. |
| Single primary CTA on every page | "MESSAGE STUDIO SPETIA." Identical wording, identical styling, every page. |
| Qualifying funnel | Ad/Search → Home → Primary CTA → WhatsApp auto-reply with qualifying questions → Sangeetha or Sathya replies within 24 hours → Site visit → Contract |
| Friction reduction | 4-field form max if used. WhatsApp pre-filled message. No multi-step flows. No login walls. No popup interrupts. No newsletter signup. |
| Social proof through proof | The work is the social proof. Project photographs carry it. No testimonial carousels. No "5-star rated" badges. |
| Real scarcity | "We start six homes a year in Bangalore" — true, not manufactured. No countdown timers. |
| Risk reversal | Stated plainly in /approach: "Sangeetha visits your plot before we quote anything. There is no charge for the visit." |
| Video for trust-heavy explanations | BBMP/BDA approval video, founder introduction video. See §11. |

### 3.2 The Hormozi tactics this site rejects

| Hormozi tactic | Why Studio Spetia rejects it |
|---|---|
| Money-back guarantees | Reads marketplace-y in architecture. The guarantee is the named architect's reputation. |
| Value-stacking ("you get this AND this AND this — total value ₹X — yours for ₹Y") | DEEJOS does this. Brick & Bolt does this. Studio Spetia does not. |
| Manufactured urgency (countdown timers, "only X spots left") | Real scarcity beats fake urgency at this tier. |
| Free lead magnets ("download our 50-point checklist") | Studio Spetia has nothing to download. The conversation is the magnet. |
| Stat-block bombardment | DEEJOS' signature move. Studio Spetia uses one stat sentence in prose. |
| Calculator UX ("Get your instant quote") | Marketplace tell. Pricing arrives in WhatsApp after qualification. |
| Multi-city expansion claims | Studio Spetia is Bangalore only and proud of it. |

### 3.3 The editorial vocabulary on top of Hormozi mechanics

- Mysore Ivory ground (#F1ECE2) — never pure white.
- GT Sectra Display Light for headlines, sentence case.
- ITF Mier B Book for body, 1.65 line-height, max 65 characters per line.
- Single accent: Hampi Terracotta (#9A4A2E), maximum 5% of any surface.
- One photograph per section, full-bleed when it earns it.
- 1px ink hairlines (40px wide, never full-container) as section dividers.
- ▪ end-marks at the close of standalone passages.
- Zero icons used decoratively.
- Zero gradients, zero drop-shadows, zero glows.

## 5. The 2026 design grammar

- **Editorial typography as primary architecture.** Viewport-scaled type. GT Sectra Display Light at clamp(2.5rem, 8vw, 4.5rem).
- **Tactile brutalism replacing soft UI.** Zero drop shadows, zero gradients, max 2px border radius.
- **Curated calm over superficial opulence.** Restraint is the differentiator.
- **Anti-AI human-touch.** Hand-drawn sketches, candid photography.
- **Refined serif fonts as luxury signal.** GT Sectra Display Light is the locked headline face.
- **Bold minimalism.** Each section has one dominant element.
- **Bento grid paradox — to be avoided.** Linear vertical narrative on mobile.
- **Sustainability as brand signal.** Lighthouse 95+. Page weight under 1.2 MB.

**Trends explicitly rejected:** Y2K aesthetic, maximalism, 3D/AR features, WebGL particles, Lottie animations, bento grids, glassmorphism, mixed-media collage, dark mode.

## 9. Page-by-page specification

### 9.1 / (Home) — the conversion page

The most important page. Does 90% of conversion work.

**Section 1: Header (sticky desktop, hide-on-scroll mobile)**

- **Top-centered wordmark:** "Studio" stacked over "SPETIA," SPETIA dominant. Ink color. ~80px wide mobile, ~120px desktop.
- **Left of wordmark:** "= MENU" — Mier B Book, 13px, all caps, tracked +0.12em.
- **Right of wordmark:** "MESSAGE STUDIO SPETIA" — same styling, terracotta on hover.
- **Background:** ivory.
- **Bottom edge:** 1px ink hairline at 20% opacity.
- **Sub-navigation row below header:** Studio · Work · Approach · Contact — sentence case, ink, no underlines. 1px hairline above and below.

## 17. The "is this AI?" sniff test

Run this test before shipping each page.

### 17.1 Copy-level (8 questions)

For each section's copy:

1. **Does this sentence contain a specific noun that could not be auto-generated?** (A neem tree. The south wall. Malleshwaram. Eighteen months. Sangeetha's hand.) If zero specific nouns, rewrite.

2. **Could this sentence appear, identically, on the website of a yoga studio, a coaching practice, or a SaaS startup?** If yes, generic AI copy. Rewrite with specificity.

3. **Does this sentence make a claim that requires evidence, without providing the evidence?** ("Premium quality.") If yes, replace with the evidence ("We rebuilt the south wall twice.")

4. **Does this paragraph use more than one adjective per sentence?** If yes, delete the weakest adjective.

5. **Could a 45-year-old IT director in Sarjapur read this and think "this sounds like marketing"?** If yes, rewrite. The voice should sound like an architect explaining her practice in person.

6. **Is there an em-dash or semicolon doing decorative work?** If yes, remove. Use periods.

7. **Is the sentence longer than 25 words?** If yes, break it into two.

8. **Does the sentence contain "we believe," "we are passionate about," "we are committed to"?** If yes, delete the entire sentence.

### 17.2 Component-level (7 questions)

For each visual element:

1. **Is this layout something seen on Vercel's home page, Stripe's home page, or any AI tool's marketing site in the last six months?** If yes, rebuild from a different reference (Khosla, Architecture Brio, Cereal Magazine, Aman, MUJI).

2. **Does this section contain three or four boxes side-by-side, each with an icon and small heading?** If yes, replace with prose paragraph.

3. **Are corners of cards rounded at 8px, 12px, or 16px?** If yes, change to 0px or 2px max.

4. **Is there a soft shadow under any element?** If yes, remove.

5. **Is there a button labeled "Get Started" or "Learn More"?** If yes, replace with "Message Studio Spetia."

6. **Does the page have a "Hero → Features → Stats → Testimonials → CTA" flow?** If yes, that is the SaaS marketing template. The Studio Spetia flow is "Hero → Proposition → Work → Studio → Approach → Tiers → Capacity → CTA."

7. **Are all images the same aspect ratio in a perfect grid?** If yes, vary.

### 17.3 The single most useful test

Ask the AI tool that built this: **"If you removed the words 'Studio Spetia' from this site, would the site still feel like a Bangalore residential design+build studio, or would it feel like a SaaS landing page with construction photos?"**

If the answer is the second, the site is AI-flavored and needs rebuilding.

## 19. The single most important rule (restated)

**Default to subtraction.**

If at any point during development the AI tool building this site is uncertain whether to add an element, add a feature, or "improve" the design — remove instead.

Studio Spetia is built on the discipline of doing less, more deliberately. The brand is the things we choose not to put on the page. The website should feel almost too quiet on the first scroll, until the visitor reads the headline carefully and realizes the proposition is more interesting than they expected.

The visitor's experience should be:

- Second 1: "This is calm."
- Second 5: "Wait, who is this?"
- Second 10: "Twenty years, eighty homes — that's serious."
- Second 30: "An architect mother and an engineer son? Together?"
- Second 60: "I should message them."

Five seconds in, the visitor should not yet know if Studio Spetia is for them. Sixty seconds in, they should know they want to find out.

That's the brief. Build it.
